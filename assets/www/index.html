What is Cervical Cancer?

Staging

As we all know that cervical cancer has some staging characteristics that can explain how danger the cancer has spread in the body. Here we review from a journal from Benedet (2000) what kinds of stages in cervical cancer based on clinical evaluation.

Stage 0


This stage involve the thickness of the epithelium with atypical cell but there is no signs of invasion into the stroma.

Stage 1

There are two types of this stage I, stage IA and IB. Then stage IA is divided into two, stage IA1 and IA2 with little bit of differences on stromal invasion. The diagnostic for stage IA can be known only by microscopy examination where the invasion should not be >5 mm taken from the base of epithelium, either surface or glandular. The diagnose for stage IA1 is when the invasion no greater than 3.0 mm in depth and 7.0 mm or less in horizontal spread. Meanwhile for the stage IA2, the stromal invasion more than 3.0 mm in depth and not more than 5.0 mm with a horizontal spread 7.0 mm or less.

Stage IB divided into two just like stage IA, stage IB1 and IB2. The diagnostic point for stage IB is they has larger lesions than stage IA. For stage IB1, the lesion is clinically visible 4 cm and the stage IB2 is more than 4 cm in greatest dimension.

Stage 2


A patient with tumour invading beyond the uterus but not to pelvic wall should be classified to stage II. This stage is divide into two, stage IIA and stage IIB with the differences in parametrial invasion. In stage IIA the characteristics of tumour invading without parametrial invasion and in stage IIB without parametrial invasion.

Stage 3


The presence of non functioning kidney in patient is the diagnostic for stage III and divided into two, stage IIIA and stage IIIB. Tumour in stage IIIA involves lower third of vagina no extension to pelvic wall. Meanwhile in stage IIIB, the tumour extends to pelvic wall and/or causes non functioning kidney.

Stage 4


The presence of bullous edema is a sign for stage IV. Stage IVA is diagnostic when the tumour invades mucosa of bladder or rectum and/or extends beyond true pelvis. And the last stage is stage IVB when there is distant metastasis in the patient.

Causes

Many factors in our enviroment life can affect our health especially our reproductive’s organ. From an American Cancer Society’s article, we review some risk factors that can cause the development of cervical cancer.

HPV Infection


Human Pappiloma Virus is the most important and common risk factor for cervical cancer is infection caused by human papilloma virus. HPV is a virus that has more than 150 related viruses and some of them can cause a type of growth called papilloma. This virus can infect cells on the surface of the skin and in the genitals but not the blood or internal organs sech as the heart or lungs. HPV can be passed from one person to another especially during sex or skin-to-skin contact. Certain types of HPV may cause different warts to appear on or around the genital organs. The high-risk types that strongly linked to cancer and most common risk types include HPV 16 and HPV 18, which is become an interest topic in our project.

Smoking


Woman who smoke can get higher risk to get cervical cancer than woman who don’t smoke. Some substances or chemicals can affect organs other than lungs because they were absorbed through the lungs and carried in the bloodstream through the body. Tobacco have been found in the cervical mucus of women who smoke because the researchers believe that these substances damage the DNA of cervix cells. The immune system from a woman who smoke can be less effective in fighting HPV infections.

Immunosuppression

Human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) is the virus that cause AIDS, may damages the immune system and puts women at higher risk for HPV infection. For women with an impaired immune system from HIV might develop into an invasive cancer faster than it normally would. Another example of women at risk of cervical cancer are woman that taking drugs to suppress their immune response, especially for those who being treated for an autoimmune disease or those who have had an organ transplant.

Chlamydia infection


Chlamydia is a kind of bacteria that can affect the reproductive system and acan cause pelvic inflammation then leading to infertility. Woman who are infected with chlamydia often have no symptoms and has higher risk of cervical cancer.

Oral contraceptives


The risk of cervical cancer goes up if the longer a woman takes Ocs, but the risk goes back down again after Ocs are stopped.

Full-term pregnancies

Woman who have had 3 or more full-term pregnancies have an increased risk of developing cervical cancer. These woman have had unprotected intercourse to get pregnant, so they may have had more exposure to HPV. Also, hormonal changes during pregnancy as possibly making women more susceptible to HPV infection or cancer growth.

Diethylstilbestrol (DES)


DES is a hormonal drug that was consume to some women to prevent miscarriage between 1940 and 1971. These women develop clear cell adenocarcinoma of the vagina or cervix more often than would normally be expected. The average age of women ehen they are diagnosed with DES-related clear-cell adenocarcinoma is 19 years old. DES daughters may also be at increased risk of developing squamous cell cancers and pre-cancers of the cervix linked to HPV.

Family history of ca cervix

If a woman’s mother or sister or any other family had cervical cancer, her chances of developing the disease are 2 to 3 times higher than if no one in the family had it. Inherited condition in a family history can affect others member, especially women in that family.

Prevent From Cervical Cancer

HPV VACCINE


in USA, this vaccines were licensed in 2006 for use in females aged 9 to 26 years old. The vaccine can be used toreduce the incidence of precancerous cervical lesions caused by HPV type 16 and 18 especially for those who haven’t previously been infected with these strains.

DON’T SMOKE


tobacco by-products have been found in the cervical mucus of women who smoke and smoking makes the immune system less effective in HPV infections.

USE CONDOM


condoms can give protection against HPV but they don’t completely prevent infection because they don’t cover completely every posibble HPV-infected area of the body. Just try to be loyal for your partner and it can prevent you from getting HPV infection.

Cervical Biopsies

Back

Colposcopy


This exam need to be done with the doctor when the colposcope (an instrument that has magnifying lenses) used to see the surface of the cervix closely and clearly.

Cone biopsy


The doctor removes a cone-shaped piece of tissue from the cervix and this biopsy can be used as treatment to remove many pre-cancers and some very early cancers.

Endocervical scraping


if the area of HPV infection and pre-cancer can’t be seen with the colposcope, there are another method which is using curette to scrape the inside of the canal to take some tissue and then examine it in lab

Punch Biopsy


The doctor uses a sharp tool to take or pinch a small part for samples of cervical tissue.

LEEP


Loop Electrosurgical Excision Procedure : the doctor uses an electric wire loop to slice or cut off a thin and round shape of cervical tissue.

TV

Back

vaccines can be used too for treatment for this cancer and there are many types of this theraupetic vaccines.

Protein/peptide-based vaccines


This vaccines will contain some protein or peptide where it can enhanced the immune systems of the body and eventhough this vaccines has low immunogenicity, adjuvants (agent delivery systems) immune will help them to enhance vaccine potency. This vaccines are easy to produce, stable, and safe.

DNA/RNA-based vaccines


This type of vaccines has DNA or RNA on it to deliver to the body with the help of adjuvants or gene gun. This vaccines are relatively safe, stable, cost efficient, and able to sustain reasonable levels of antigen expression within cells. They can be delivered with little risk to individuals who are immunosuppressed.

Live vector-based vaccines

This vaccines are highly immunogenic with minimal toxicity and extremely efficient infection rates. Attenuated bacterial vector-based vaccines have been explored in HPV therapeutic vaccines.

Tumor cell-based vaccines

The use of this vaccines is an interesting strategy because it is not necessary to clearly identify the tumor antigens, but this vaccines are costly and difficult to produce on large scale.

Dendritic cell-based vaccines


Aim to enhance cell immunity with the help of adjuvant but this vaccines is expensive.
